I majored in Literature and Visual Arts at Duke University, where I did literature research and completed challenging art and design projects. After graduating from college, I tutored reading and writing for college entrance exams, applied for technical writing and copy editing work, and took a web development internship with a local startup.

I got interested in web development because it rewarded patience and attention to detail and because I was excited about the potential of open source, data visualization, and using code to build educational tools. Working on real projects where I could use my writing and visual arts skills as well as my technical inclinations convinced me that I wanted to pursue a career involving web development. Inspired by this, I recently completed the Web Development Immersive program at General Assembly in Austin, TX, where I learned full-stack development.

On my free time, I enjoy watching basketball and soccer, reading and writing, listening to music, and exercising. I also like to change what I put here.
